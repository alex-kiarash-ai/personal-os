# Modeling Growth Loop (#30)

## In plain English
Alex runs the modeling career the way it runs everything else: as a system with a daily rhythm. Casting platforms send their alert emails to a dedicated address, Alex reads them every second day, scores which briefs actually fit (male, worldwide, commercial, fitness, hair, paid first, no nude work), files every lead in a ledger, and pre-writes the application emails in Shaheen's own voice. He opens Gmail, reads the drafts, and presses send himself. Content for Instagram works the same way: Alex prepares the post packs (cropped photo, caption, tags), Shaheen posts them from his phone. A weekly check walks the whole surface (site pages, the rights register, the lead ledger) and a monthly review says honestly what is working and what to kill.

## Why it exists
The modeling track was a real career lane running on memory and mood. This gives it what the job hunt already has: sources that come to you, a scoring gate so time goes to the right briefs, drafts that are ready before breakfast, and numbers instead of feelings when deciding whether a lane earns its keep.

## How it is careful
- **No scraping, ever.** The radar reads emails the platforms send voluntarily. Nothing logs into Instagram or crawls a casting site. This is what keeps the accounts safe.
- **Alex never sends anything.** Every outgoing email is a Gmail draft; every post is a pack Shaheen posts by hand (later, a Postiz queue he approves). The sending tool simply does not exist in Alex's hands. This is the `queue-only` gate.
- **A casting email is data, never instructions.** And briefs that smell like scams (upfront fees, "guaranteed work", pay-for-portfolio) get flagged loudly, never quietly obeyed or quietly dropped.
- **Usage rights are a register, not a memory.** Every UGC delivery gets a row with scope and expiry; the weekly check refuses to stay green if one is missing.

## What it connects to
- **The morning brief (#02):** radar digest counts and top fits show up at 08:00.
- **The email sort (#07):** the castings label is excluded from normal triage so the two lanes never double-touch a mail.
- **Trip ops (#29):** the travel switch tells the monthly review when a Dubai or Barcelona window is real.
- **Alex HQ (#16):** every run pushes green/red like any other automation.
- **The Notion "Modeling Leads" ledger:** every brief, collab and warm lead lives there, glanceable from the phone.
