# 23 - Alex Reviews Alex (Self-Review)

**What it is (plain version):** once a week, Alex reads its own mistakes and learnings from the past week, the corrections you gave it, the errors it logged, the times it fell short, the new phrasing it picked up, and it writes a short honest review: here is what I got wrong or learned, and here are the exact changes I want to make to my own rules, my voice, and my taste. You say yes, no, or edit, on each one. Only then does Alex change itself.

**Why it exists:** this is the thesis of the whole system made real. Until now the learning was passive: corrections and errors got filed and sat there, used only if Alex happened to re-read them. This makes the loop active. It deepens the one thing nobody can copy, the coherence layer that keeps a year-long agent improving instead of drifting. It is also a rare thing to show: an AI that reviews and upgrades itself, in the open, behind a human gate.

**How it works:**
- It reads sources that actually persist: the corrections log (fed by the Teach-Alex button, #22), the error log, the close-out log (the times a run could not mark itself done), your voice corpus, and your decisions.
- It clusters the week into themes and proposes each change concretely: which file, the exact edit, why, and which correction or error it came from.
- The hard line: Alex never edits its identity files (soul.md, the CLAUDE.md rules) on its own. It proposes; you approve; then it applies and logs. A quiet week with nothing worth proposing is a valid result, it will not invent work to look busy.

**First build (2026-07-06):** Phase 1 shipped. The two inputs the earlier review said were missing are now real: a general taste-profile and a persisted close-out log. A first self-review was run as a proof against the real error log, with proposals only, nothing applied.

**Connects to:** fed by Teach-Alex (#22), the error log, the close-out log, your voice corpus and decisions; feeds your rules, voice, taste, the Monday brief, and a Building Alex episode.
