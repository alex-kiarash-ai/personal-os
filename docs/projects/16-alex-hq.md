# 16 - Alex HQ

## What it actually does
The glanceable dashboard: **https://hq.shaheenkiarash.com** (private, password-protected), built as a phone-installable web app in the Building Alex visual style. One screen shows the numbers that matter: daily tiles (jobs processed, drafts, inbox stats, brief status), weekly tiles, infrastructure tiles - and the flagship **automation health board**: every automation's last run, green/red status, and staleness (a daily job that hasn't reported in two days turns amber by itself). It's two-way: a "Drop a note to Alex" card accepts typed or voice notes any time; Alex collects and files them into the vault at its next touchpoint. Local numbers only this laptop can see (MCP tool count, vault size, scheduler health, backup age) are pushed up daily by the `/alex-hq` command.

Since 2026-07-06 there's a dedicated **"n8n · broken today"** card: it answers "is anything on the Hetzner box down right now?" in one glance. Calm and grey when all is well; a red number that names the culprit the instant any workflow fails (a workflow either erroring, or a daily one going quiet for more than a day). It goes red immediately - the moment a workflow throws, the error handler flips this card - and clears itself once the next health check confirms things are healthy again. Tapping it lists every active workflow on the box with when it last ran and what, if anything, is broken.

The same day brought a refinement round on the whole dashboard: real date-and-time stamps everywhere instead of "2h ago" guesswork, three new life cards - **To-Do** (the open items from the Notion build board), **gym today** (GYM or REST, computed from the every-second-day cycle), and **plants due** (which of the 8 houseplants need water, computed live from the watering log) - plus a next-booking line on the Airbnb tile, a by-category split behind the expenses tile, and a calmer look (slow drifting background, fewer mini-charts, no "tap for breakdown" labels).

On 2026-07-07 the health board was fixed to show the **full project roster**. It had been listing only the ~15 automations that push live numbers, so the roughly ten projects that run on demand or sit dormant were simply missing - the board looked out of date. Now it reads the master project registry directly, so every one of the 25 registered projects appears. The ones that report show their live status and last-run time as before; the quieter ones (on-demand tools, event-triggered helpers, dormant or parked projects) show a hollow marker and an honest label like "on-demand" or "dormant" instead of a fake alarm, and tapping one explains what it is and why it's quiet. Because the board is now driven by the registry, adding a new project makes it appear on HQ automatically - no dashboard edit needed.

On 2026-07-13 a three-reviewer design audit of the live page turned into a 3-wave overhaul plan, and wave 1 was built, deployed and live-verified the same evening (12 fixes). The two that matter most: the phone app now **refreshes itself when you come back to it** - before, reopening HQ in the morning quietly showed last night's numbers with the clock frozen - and the phone screen's first view now shows what you need to act on (the waiting-on-you bar, the note box, and the first alert tiles) instead of spending it on already-filed note history. Smaller honesty fixes rode along: a stalled health tile now dims its number and says which day it's really from instead of shouting a stale value, the health board calls its debug feeds by real names ("Claude Quota", not `quota`), the waiting bar's "oldest 29d" age badge works again, and the quiet two-thirds of the project list folds away on the phone until tapped.

Wave 3, live since 2026-07-14, is the behavior wave - how the page moves and how you leave it - and it completes the overhaul. Three fixes. First, **motion now respects you**: if your phone or PC is set to "reduce motion", the dashboard goes completely still (no drifting background, no pulsing dots, no shimmer), and for everyone else the numbers no longer "count up" from zero on every open - the first thing you see IS the real value, with the count-up saved for the moment a number actually changes while you watch. And on a bad day, instead of nine red dots all pulsing in sync like a light show, only the worst dot in each section keeps the heartbeat; the other reds hold a steady alarm. Second, **one voice for numbers**: the big figures inside the drill-down panels used to render in a different typeface than the tiles two inches above them; every data numeral now speaks the same instrument font, and the brand rulebook was amended to say exactly that. Third, and the one you'll feel most: **the phone's Back gesture now closes a drill-down instead of quitting the whole app** - previously the most natural swipe in the world threw you out of HQ entirely.

Wave 2 followed the same night and is live. This is the wave that **fixes the alarm language**. Before, one bad day painted the whole top of the page maroon: a red tile's tint washed across its entire face, so a stale phone sync shouted as loudly as two dead job engines. Now a red tile keeps a calm dark face and carries its alarm in the thin ring, the status dot and the small red title - only the "waiting on you" bar is still allowed to go full red, because a critical item there IS the loudest fact on the page. Status dots got shapes as well as colors (red = solid and pulsing, amber = an open ring), so they stay tellable apart even for color-blind eyes or a greyscale screen. Counts that mean "you need to act" (urgent inbox items, broken workflows) now show their number in a burnt orange instead of plain white - zeros whisper, actionable numbers burn. The Applications card finally uses its double width to show the real backlog ("ready to apply BI 27 · AI 69") in bright numerals instead of hiding it in a grey footnote, dropped notes can wrap onto a second line instead of getting chopped, and the section labels and grid were straightened so every row fills cleanly on a big screen.

## Why it exists
Sixteen automations produce numbers in sixteen places; the question "is everything actually working?" had no single answer. HQ is that answer, sized for a phone glance at a bus stop. The health board is the real point - automations fail silently by default, and this makes silence visible. The note-drop exists because thoughts arrive when Alex isn't running; now capture is always on. Deliberately: no Notion database, no chat - a dashboard and an inbox, nothing pretending to be a conversation.

## Works together with
- **Every automation** - all push run metrics to it (the "metrics contract"); it is the system's shared nervous system.
- **The n8n backend** - four workflows documented in [docs/n8n/](../n8n/): [metrics ingest](../n8n/hq-metrics-ingest/), [summary API](../n8n/hq-summary-api/), [pipeline stats sidecar](../n8n/hq-pipeline-stats/), [notes inbox](../n8n/hq-notes-inbox/).
- **[Pipeline Error Alert](../n8n/pipeline-error-alert/)** - the box's smoke alarm now feeds HQ directly: it guards all active workflows and flips the "n8n · broken today" card red on any failure.
- **[Morning Brief](02-morning-brief.md)** - quotes HQ health; collects dropped notes; the ≤24h backstop that reads the same failure alerts from the Notion side.
- **The Recovery layer** - the backup job and future integrity checker report their status here.
